Summary:
This classification is broadly consistent with how macro-driven liquidity impacts crypto markets like ETHUSDT, but it mixes **scheduled data releases, policy events, and real-time market indicators**, which serve different roles. The hierarchy is directionally correct, but needs slight refinement in how “impact” is interpreted (immediate volatility vs structural regime shift).

## 1. Validating the “Extreme Tier” (Highest Impact)

Your list:

* NFP
* CPI / Core CPI
* FOMC decision
* Powell press conference
* 10Y Treasury yield
* DXY (Dollar Index)

### Assessment

This grouping is **accurate and aligned with institutional macro trading frameworks**.

* **CPI / Core CPI** → Primary inflation signal; strongest driver of Fed expectations
* **NFP** → Labor strength proxy; second most important inflation-pressure input
* **FOMC + Powell presser** → Direct policy signaling (often overrides data)
* **10Y yield** → “Real-time discount rate” for all risk assets
* **DXY** → Liquidity and USD strength proxy affecting global risk appetite

### Key nuance

* CPI and FOMC events cause **regime shifts** (repricing trend changes)
* Powell pressers often cause **second-leg volatility after the decision**
* 10Y and DXY are **transmission channels**, not “events”

---

## 2. Real-time indicators (your “live filters” idea)

* DXY
* 10Y yield
* VIX
* Fed Funds Futures

### Assessment

This is **conceptually strong and aligns with professional risk desks**.

They are not “news events,” but:

* **DXY rising during session → liquidity tightening signal**
* **10Y yield rising → discount rate compression on crypto**
* **VIX rising → cross-asset risk-off positioning**
* **Fed Funds Futures → forward pricing of policy (expectation engine)**

### Key refinement

These are better classified as:

> **Macro regime indicators (continuous inputs), not ranked events**

They do not “shock” price like CPI, but they determine whether setups like SMC, FVG, or BOS are *valid or fragile*.

---

## 3. Treasury Auctions (your rank 45 point)

Your claim:

> Weak 10Y/30Y auction spikes yields and flows into crypto selling within minutes

### Assessment: Partially correct, but context-dependent

* Auctions affect **term premium and bond market liquidity**
* Weak demand can push yields up quickly (especially 10Y)
* Crypto reaction depends on:

  * existing positioning
  * macro sensitivity regime (risk-on vs risk-off)
  * concurrent USD liquidity conditions

### Important nuance

* Auction impact is usually **short-lived unless it reinforces a broader yield trend**
* It is more of a **confirmation catalyst**, not a primary driver

So your ranking of “low attention but high surprise impact” is fair.

## 4. Structural correction to your framework

Your system mixes three different layers:

### A) High-impact macro shocks (invalidate setups)

* CPI / Core CPI
* NFP
* FOMC + Powell

### B) Transmission variables (trend drivers)

* 10Y yield
* DXY

### C) Continuous sentiment / positioning (filters)

* VIX
* Fed Funds Futures
* (plus crypto funding rates, though not in your list)

### D) Micro catalysts (often overlooked)

* Treasury auctions
* Jobless claims
* PPI

---

## 5. Key takeaway for ETHUSDT trading logic

A more precise institutional framing:

* **CPI / FOMC = regime breakers (setup invalidation risk)**
* **NFP = directional volatility trigger (trend acceleration or reversal)**
* **DXY + 10Y = structure of liquidity (decides whether SMC works)**
* **VIX / futures = positioning pressure (risk appetite gauge)**
* **Auctions / claims / PPI = secondary confirmation signals**

---

## 6. Final evaluation of your model

Strengths:

* Correctly identifies “macro shock hierarchy”
* Correctly separates scheduled vs real-time variables
* Correctly links yields/DXY to crypto sensitivity

Limitations:

* Treats all items as a single ranked list when they operate in different mechanisms
* Overestimates auction impact in isolation
* Understates role of positioning (futures, perp funding, liquidity depth)
